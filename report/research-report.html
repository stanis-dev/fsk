Fact-check corrections
  • refuted Claim 11 (France): SIGN FR launched July 31, 2025; Feb 2025 Finance Act ended self-attestation; POS vendors needed third-party certification (LNE/Infocert) initiated by Aug 31, 2025, mandatory March 1, 2026
    The historical parts are accurate but the 'mandatory March 1, 2026' endpoint is wrong as of June 2026 — the mandate was first postponed, then repealed. CONFIRMED: Art. 43 of the Loi de finances 2025 removed the editor self-attestation option effective Feb 16, 2025, and publishers had to sign a certification engagement with an accredited body before Aug 31, 2025 (LNE: https://www.lne.fr/fr/actualites/2025/logiciels-caisses-suppression-auto-attestation-conformite-fiscale; BOFiP). REFUTED: certification never became mandatory on March 1, 2026. (1) DGFiP postponed the certificate-obtention deadline from March 1, 2026 to September 1, 2026 via BOFiP update ACTU-2025-00160 published Oct 1, 2025 (https://bofip.impots.gouv.fr/bofip/14826-PGP.html/ACTU-2025-00160), citing certification-body backlog. (2) Art. 125 of the Loi de finances pour 2026 then REVERSED the whole reform, re-establishing editor self-attestation as valid proof of compliance, applicable from Feb 21, 2026 (https://www.legifiscal.fr/actualites-fiscales/4460-loi-finances-2026-retablissement-auto-certification-logiciels-caisse.html; https://entreprendre.service-public.gouv.fr/actualites/A18087?lang=fr). Correct fact: as of June 2026, third-party NF525/LNE certification is no longer mandatory in France — self-attestation is again permitted — which undermines the stated regulatory driver for SIGN FR. The July 31, 2025 launch date itself rests only on fiskaly's own blog (not independently contradicted).

Italian fiscalization landscape

Why SIGN IT is timely: the 2027 software-fiscalization window and the sanctions that price non-compliance.

Italy's fiscalization regime is at an inflection point in mid-2026. Since 2019-2020, retail receipts (corrispettivi) have required either a hardware Registratore Telematico (RT) or the AdE's web procedure ("Documento Commerciale on line"); art. 24 of D.Lgs. 1/2024 created a legal fourth channel — a certified pure-software solution — operationalized by Agenzia delle Entrate Provvedimento n. 111204 of 7 March 2025, which defines a PEM/PEL architecture (fiscal modules MF1/MF2), a producer/erogatore accreditation model, third-party certification, and approval by the Commission for fiscal measuring devices. Technical specs have iterated rapidly (v1.0 Feb 2025 → v1.2 on 2 Dec 2025 adding ISO 9001/27001 requirements → v1.3 current, with annexes updated 17 April 2026); a 27 May 2026 working group (AdE, Sogei, AssoSoftware, certifiers) confirmed full market operation of certified software solutions from 2027, with no approved solutions yet publicly listed and fiskaly publicly in the certification pipeline for SIGN IT. Separately, the 2025 Budget Law (L. 207/2024, art. 1 commi 74-77) made POS-RT linkage mandatory from 1 January 2026: implemented by Provvedimento n. 424470 of 31 October 2025 as a purely logical (web-based) association of POS Terminal IDs to RT serial numbers in the Fatture e Corrispettivi portal, live since 5 March 2026 with a 45-day window (deadline 20 April 2026) for pre-existing devices and rolling monthly windows thereafter; sanctions are €1,000-4,000 plus possible license suspension. The receipt lottery continues weekly/monthly/annual draws for cashless purchases with an 8-char lottery code on the documento commerciale, while the instant lottery (DL 36/2022) remains dormant despite ~1.6M RTs being adapted by the 2 Oct 2023 deadline, because the ADM-AdE implementing decree/prize funding never materialized. Core penalties for memorization/transmission failures are 70% of VAT (min €300) plus business-suspension accessories, and €100 per late/omitted transmission (capped €1,000/quarter) for formal violations. Market context: ~1.7M RTs serving 1.5M+ merchants, ~80% reaching end-of-life by 2027 (fiskaly/Format Research), which is the commercial window fiskaly's SIGN IT (cloud fiscalization) and SUBMIT IT (POS-RT linkage automation) target. For integrators, the pain points are the multi-actor certification chain, evolving specs, daily-closing/12-day transmission discipline, offline/malfunction emergency procedures, returns/voids document types, lottery code handling, and now POS Terminal-ID lifecycle management — exactly the complexity that documentation quality must absorb.

Full detail notes

1. The corrispettivi telematici regime — channels as of mid-2026

Legal foundation: art. 2, comma 1, D.Lgs. 5 agosto 2015, n. 127 — obligation of electronic memorization and telematic transmission of daily corrispettivi (in force for all merchants since 1 Jan 2020; larger merchants 1 July 2019). Certification instrument output is the documento commerciale (D.M. 7 dicembre 2016), not the old paper scontrino fiscale.

Four channels (confirmed by the 27 May 2026 AdE/Sogei/AssoSoftware working group — Studio Pizzano, Studio PAA, 29 May 2026):

  1. Hardware Registratore Telematico (RT) / Server RT — certified fiscal device with fiscal memory, sealed by authorized technicians (laboratori abilitati), periodic biennial verification.
  2. Web procedure "Documento Commerciale on line" in the AdE Fatture e Corrispettivi portal (per-transaction manual entry; what fiskaly SIGN IT lite automates).
  3. RT with BLOB technology (variant referenced in the 2026 roadmap discussions).
  4. NEW: certified Soluzione Software (SSW) — pure software, no fiscal hardware. Legal basis art. 24, D.Lgs. 8 gennaio 2024, n. 1 ("decreto Adempimenti", in force January 2024). Full market operation from 2027.

2. The software solution (SSW) — provvedimenti, architecture, certification

Regulatory timeline (precise)

  • 8 Jan 2024 — D.Lgs. 1/2024, art. 24: creates the legal option of software-only memorization/transmission (fiskaly blog).
  • July 2024 — AdE notified the draft to the EU Commission (TRIS technical-regulation procedure) (Agenda Digitale).
  • 7 Mar 2025 — Provvedimento n. 111204/2025 (Prot. AGEDC001.111204): defines information to transmit + technical specs for realization, approval and release of SSW (AdE provvedimento page; PDF). Same day, provvedimento n. 33796/2025 updated the general corrispettivi technical specs (Indicom).
  • Spec versions: v1.0 Feb 2025 (allegato to 111204); v1.1 mid-2025 (AdE); v1.2 published 2 Dec 2025 — added ISO certification requirements for erogatori (AdE PDF, timestamp Dec 2025); v1.3 current, annexes updated 17 April 2026 (AdE spec page).
  • 25 Feb 2026 — AdE published an updated list of certification bodies (enti certificatori) for software solutions (fiskaly certification-requirements blog).
  • 27 May 2026 — working group AdE + Sogei + AssoSoftware + certifiers: roadmap to 2027 confirmed; pilot positive, no feasibility objections; no count of approved solutions disclosed — no approved solution publicly listed yet as of June 2026.

Architecture (PEM/PEL, MF1/MF2)

  • PEM (Punto di Emissione): merchant-side device (PC/tablet/smartphone/SmartPOS) running MF1 (Modulo Fiscale 1): secure transaction recording, documento commerciale emission, payment-data capture, lottery management. Census + activation flow ("Censimento ed attivazione PEM" YAML/XSD).
  • PEL (Punto di Elaborazione): erogatore-managed backend running MF2 (Modulo Fiscale 2): validation, fiscal storage of transaction-level detail, long-term digital preservation, transmission of the daily corrispettivi XML to AdE, audit-support REST APIs exposed to AdE.
  • Integrity: hash-chained daily journal (tamper evidence), minimum 48-hour local retention at the PEM, bidirectional PEM-PEL web services, real-time audit capability for inspectors (Agenda Digitale).
  • Published artifacts on the AdE spec page: XSDs for Corrispettivi, Corrispettivi-EM, DocumentoCommerciale, Journal, Metadati, Catalogo, CensimentoPEM, AttivazionePEM-Esito, RichiestaAudit, Segnalazioni(+Esito); REST API specs (Audit APIs exposed by erogatore; APIs exposed by AdE); YAML OpenAPI files (trasmissione corrispettivi giornalieri, comunica stato PEM, censimento/attivazione PEM, audit journal/documento commerciale); SBOM guidelines (Allegato-SSW-LineeGuidaSBOM); archive and audit-services guidelines.

Actors & certification chain

  • Produttore (software producer): pre-accredits with AdE; builds per specs; must hold ISO 9001 + ISO/IEC 27001 (per v1.2 requirements); submits solution to an authorized ente certificatore (independent certification body; early commentary mentioned universities/CNR-type labs); then AdE approval upon favorable opinion of the Commissione per l'approvazione degli apparecchi misuratori fiscali (art. 5, D.M. 23 marzo 1983).
  • Erogatore (service provider): accredits with AdE; operates the PEL; legally responsible for fiscal storage, correct operation, and audit availability; activates approved solutions for merchants.
  • Esercente (merchant): uses the PEM; obligations shift largely to provider side ("software responsibility over hardware compliance" — fiskaly).
  • AdE keeps a public register of accredited erogatori and approved solutions (Studio Cerbone full provvedimento text).

Open issues flagged (May 2026 working group)

  1. Producer certification timelines → market availability risk for the 2027 start.
  2. Liability allocation among producer / erogatore / merchant on malfunction.
  3. Technical specs for integrating electronic payments (POS-RT equivalent) into SSW still pending — GTN (Dec 2025) circulated industry notes on "Collegamento RT_Pos_SSW" (GTN PDF).

3. Budget Law 2025 — POS-RT linkage (what SUBMIT IT addresses)

  • Norm: L. 30 dicembre 2024, n. 207 (Legge di Bilancio 2025), art. 1, commi 74-77, effective 1 January 2026: (a) obligation to link payment instruments to the corrispettivi certification instrument; (b) obligation to memorize payment method/amount at transaction time and transmit daily, in aggregate, electronic-payment data together with corrispettivi (Baker Tilly; Studio Stefani).
  • Provvedimento attuativo: n. 424470 del 31 ottobre 2025 (FIPE; AdE press release 31/10/2025; Altalex). Linkage is logical, not physical: a web association in Fatture e Corrispettivi between RT matricola and POS identifiers. No RT replacement/adaptation needed for the association itself.
  • Operational guide published by AdE ~20 Feb 2026 (Fisco Oggi; fiskaly).
  • Official FAQ, 30 items, updated 25 March 2026 (AdE PDF) — full contents verified:
  • Web procedure available from 5 March 2026 (FAQ 3).
  • POS already held at 1 Jan 2026 or activated in January: link within 45 days from 5 March 2026 → deadline 20 April 2026 (Confesercenti).
  • Variations/new POS after 31 Jan 2026: register between the 6th day of the second month after activation and the last working day of that same month (e.g. POS activated April 2026 → 6-30 June 2026) (FAQ 12).
  • Who: anyone certifying corrispettivi via RT or the "Documento Commerciale on line" web procedure who collects electronically (FAQ 5).
  • In scope: physical POS (Terminal ID + acquirer fiscal code/denomination), SoftPOS apps, and virtual instruments (online payment platforms — identified by acquirer data only) (FAQ 14). Acquirer-reported instruments are pre-loaded into the procedure (FAQ 15); manual entry with convention contract number for missing ones (FAQ 16); franchisor-owned POS must be manually added and linked (FAQ 26).
  • Out of scope: bank transfers (FAQ 11); POS used exclusively for exempt sales (tobacco, fuel, distance sales etc.) (FAQ 9), but mixed-use POS must be linked (FAQ 7-8).
  • Many-to-many allowed: one physical POS ↔ multiple RTs at the same address; virtual POS across venues; one RT ↔ multiple POS (FAQ 18, 20). "Dispositivo ambulante" flag for itinerant traders (FAQ 21, 30). "POS non collegati" function to disclaim non-owned/returned/exempt-only POS (FAQ 22-24).

4. Lotteria degli scontrini

  • Deferred/weekly regime (active in 2026): weekly draws every Thursday (15 prizes €25k), monthly and annual (€5M) draws; cashless payment only + customer codice lotteria (8-char alphanumeric obtained at lotteriadegliscontrini.gov.it) shown at checkout; 1 virtual ticket per euro (purchases ≥ €1); RT transmits lottery data with the documento commerciale (Informazione Fiscale; official portal). Idempotency (intro doc, verbatim highlights): follows IETF draft-ietf-httpapi-idempotency-key-header-05; UUIDv4 (or v3) per logically distinct request; required for POST/PATCH, ignored for GET/PUT/DELETE; cache TTL 24h; "Exception: The POST /tokens endpoint currently does not react on the provided X-Idempotency-Key HTTP header. This exception will be resolved by upcoming improvements"; 422 = retry payload mismatch, 409 = concurrent processing; X-Idempotency-Replayed: "true"/"false" since 2026-03-18.

Pagination: token-based. Query limit (1–100, default 10) + token (Base64 index pointer); response pagination {next (URI), token, limit} — "If not provided, the current elements in the results set are the last elements." List filters (since 2026-03-18) on listLocations/listSystems/listRecords: taxpayer_id, location_id, system_id, type.

Environments / testing: test.api.fiskaly.com is a full sandbox (all accounts start in TEST); LIVE requires "Go LIVE" sales approval in HUB; TEST and LIVE are fully isolated (separate orgs, separate API keys); "Testing is not supported in the LIVE environment, as all data is transmitted directly to the authorities." Postman environment + collection downloadable (/static/postman/fiskaly_sign_it_2026-02-03_postman_{environment,collection}.json, both HTTP 200). Status page: status.fiskaly.com.

Rate limits: 429 + TooManyRequestsResponse defined on every operation, but no numeric limits, no rate-limit headers, no Retry-After documented in spec, guides, or FAQ. Changelogs admit limits exist ("improved createToken endpoint rate limit behavior", 2025-12-08/12).

Webhooks/events: none in the API. No OpenAPI callbacks/webhooks. The only mention is changelog 2026-04-20 "improved webhook request handling". No webhook documentation exists on the developer portal; SIGN IT also has no dashboard section yet ("fiskaly is working on a new Dashboard that will include SIGN IT data" — support FAQ). Polling GET /records/{id} is the only state-observation mechanism.

AdE outage handling (connection_loss guide): paper document at sale time; e-invoice within 12 days with receipt note "Portal unavailable", date/time, consecutive invoice number, consumer name+fiscal code, VAT number+legal name, totals before/after VAT. Retry semantics for the API itself: only the idempotency section (no backoff guidance, no guidance on what to do when a record lands in FAILED — e.g. whether to re-submit as a new record).

6. Docs quality assessment

Genuinely good

  • Service-level semantics are unusually well documented for a fiscalization API: CalVer versioning policy, a real IETF-aligned idempotency contract (TTL, 409/422 retry semantics, replay header), token-based pagination with bounds, environments policy.
  • State machines documented twice: enum-level descriptions inside the spec AND dedicated guide pages with diagrams (/static/docs/entity_system_state_mode_en_it.png, record_state_mode_en.png) labeling PATCH-driven vs INTERNAL transitions — including honesty about unimplemented DEGRADED mode.
  • Compliance/edge-case guides: AdE outage procedure (12-day e-invoice fallback), Fisconline credential expiry (90 days, renew every 60, SPID unsupported, mandatory first manual portal login), receipt lottery (deferred supported, instant not), audit handling, glossary, regulatory background (D.lgs 127/2015, Art. 24 D.lgs 1/2024).
  • Transparency features: raw AdE request/response exposed base64 in transmission; per-record signed journal + JWS file integrity; X-Trace-Identifier on every response for support escalation.
  • Detailed, dated changelog (weekly cadence, embedded in the API reference; RSS/Atom/JSON feeds on the 2024 page) and clear cross-version migration articles on support.fiskaly.com.
  • Quick start: downloadable Postman collection + environment; step-by-step integration guide with the full org bootstrap chain and a YouTube video; localized guides (de/es/fr/it).
  • Strict schemas: additionalProperties: false nearly everywhere, regex patterns, length bounds, discriminated unions — Redoc renders usable auto-generated payload samples (onlyRequiredInSamples: true); spec download button enabled.

Missing or weak

  • 168 unresolved description placeholders in the IT rendering (e.g. ((<oas_components_schemas_italian_fiscalization_description)), oas_components_responses_locations_response_description, oas_components_parameters_trace_identifier_description, abort_transaction, intention_record, journal_signature, compliance_*…). The live page silently hides them (verified: 0 literal placeholders in rendered DOM) — so key schemas like ItalianFiscalization, IntentionRecord, Journal.signature, Compliance render with blank descriptions. The 2025-08-12 rendering has 129 missing keys. This is the single biggest defect.
  • Zero request/response examples: no examples: blocks, no operation-level example, only 67 scattered field-level examples. No real Italian receipt example anywhere in the reference (the Postman collection is the only executable example).
  • Zero code samples: no x-codeSamples; no SDKs for SIGN IT (fiskaly's GitHub only has legacy DE Cloud-TSE/KassenSichV SDKs, last meaningfully updated 2022–2025; no public spec repo).
  • Error documentation is a stub: one error shape, one example code (E_UNAUTHORIZED_ACCESS). No enumeration of error codes, no per-endpoint error causes, no documentation of Record.logs message vocabulary (the changelog repeatedly says "improved error mapping", implying a rich undocumented error space). RecordLogSeverity/SystemLogSeverity enums contain only ERROR — no WARN/INFO.
  • No rate-limit documentation: numbers, scope (per key? per org?), and headers all undocumented despite 429 being on every endpoint and changelog references to createToken throttling. No Retry-After.
  • No webhooks/eventing and no documented async-completion guidance: records are processed asynchronously (PROCESSING→FINISHED) but the docs never say whether POST /records responds synchronously with the final state or whether/how to poll; no recommended polling interval or backoff.
  • Retry/failure semantics gaps: what to do after state=FAILED (re-create? same idempotency key? new document number?) is not addressed; AdE-side duplicate handling unexplained (an AbortProperties example mysteriously shows FAC-DUP-RES: 1A2B3C with no explanation).
  • PATCH /records is a documented no-op: requires a body whose schema is an empty additionalProperties: false object — endpoint exists purely for future use, which is confusing.
  • Unified-spec bleed-through: IT readers see DE/FR/BE schemas (ELSTER, MyMinFin, replay/offline headers) flagged only by emoji notes; some notes are contradictory in tone (e.g. ReceiptDetails says "Not applicable in 🇮🇹 Italy" while the integration guide centers on RECEIPT — the note refers only to the details sub-object, an easy misreading).
  • Copy/paste & QA defects: SIGN IT intro links to "Service Details: fiskaly.com/electronic-invoicing-b2b" and "Developer Guide: developer.fiskaly.com/e-invoice" (E-INVOICE product links pasted into the SIGN IT doc layer); "401.xf" typo in the Tokens tag; "improved system stabability" in changelog; a leftover dev page https://developer.fiskaly.com/api/sign-it/local returns 200 and is in the sitemap; changelog inside version 2026-02-03 documents changes through 2026-04-20 without bumping the version (mutable versioned API).
  • security granularity: everything is one JWT scheme; scopes/permissions of Subjects ("defined access rights") are asserted but never specified.
  • 2026-02-03 sitemap lacks a glossary-equivalent for taxpayer state images alt-text etc.; TaxpayerCountry is just PlainString128 (no ISO country enum) despite a CountryCode2 schema existing.

Net judgment

Above-average conceptual documentation (lifecycles, compliance context, changelog discipline) sitting on top of a below-average reference (blank descriptions from a leaky i18n templating pipeline, no examples, no error catalogue, no SDKs, undocumented limits/webhooks). An integrator can succeed using guide + Postman + Redoc-generated samples, but will hit undocumented territory exactly where production risk is highest: error codes, FAILED-record recovery, rate limits, and async timing.

API developer-experience benchmark (2026)

The frontier we measured fiskaly against: action-MCP servers, Agent Skills, llms.txt.

By mid-2026, agent-facing DX has standardized around four layers: (1) a remote, OAuth-protected MCP server that exposes both docs search and live/sandbox API actions (Stripe mcp.stripe.com, Square mcp.squareup.com, PayPal, Plaid, Vercel; Twilio's is docs-only); (2) machine-readable docs — /llms.txt with embedded agent instructions, .md versions of every page, and llms-full.txt — now auto-generated for free by docs platforms like Mintlify and ReadMe, which is why even mid-size compliance vendors (Persona, Middesk, Alloy, Sardine, Unit21, Lithic) already ship them; (3) embedded docs AI assistants (kapa.ai, Inkeep) which are table stakes; and (4) agent-executable onboarding — Stripe's claimable sandboxes (stripe sandbox create, no account needed), Agent Skills (SKILL.md, the open standard adopted by ~40 clients since Dec 2025) published at /.well-known/skills, and agent toolkits for OpenAI/Vercel/LangChain SDKs. Tier 1 — effectively free / weeks (docs-platform defaults + content discipline):

  • Postman collection + published OpenAPI spec.

Tier 2 — one small team / a quarter:
